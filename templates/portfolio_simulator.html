Metric Glossary — what do these numbers mean?
Sharpe Ratio
Risk-adjusted return: (annualised return − risk-free rate) ÷ annualised volatility. Answers "how much return do I get per unit of total risk?" Higher is better. ✦ > 1.5 excellent · > 1.0 good · > 0.5 fair · ≤ 0 poor
Sortino Ratio
Like Sharpe but only penalises downside volatility. Upward swings don't count as risk. Ideal for income ETFs that rarely spike up. Higher is better. ✦ > 2.0 excellent · > 1.5 good · > 1.0 fair
Calmar Ratio
Annual return ÷ maximum drawdown. Tells you how much return you earn per dollar of worst-case loss. Higher is better; a fund that gained 15% and only ever fell 10% scores 1.5. ✦ > 1.5 excellent · > 1.0 good · > 0.5 fair
Omega Ratio
Total probability-weighted gains above zero ÷ total losses below zero, across every daily return. Unlike Sharpe, it captures the full shape of the return distribution. ✦ > 1.5 good · > 1.0 means gains outweigh losses
Up-Capture (Up Cap)
How much of the benchmark's positive return days this holding captures. 100 % = moves in lockstep with the market on good days; > 100 % = amplifies gains. A value of 70 % means when the market rises 10 %, this holding rises ~7 %. ✦ 60–100 % is typical; > 100 % = leveraged behaviour
Down-Capture (Dn Cap)
How much of the benchmark's negative return days this holding experiences. Lower is better: a Dn Cap of 60 % means when the market falls 10 %, this holding only falls ~6 %. Defensive or low-correlation assets score well here. ✦ < 80 % excellent · < 90 % good · > 110 % amplifies losses
Ann Ret% (Annualised Return)
Compound annual growth rate over the selected analysis period, calculated from adjusted close prices. Includes price appreciation; does not add dividends on top (those are shown separately in Ann Inc / Yield%). ✦ Compare to SPY ~10 % long-run average for context
Ann Vol% (Annualised Volatility)
Standard deviation of daily returns × √252. Measures the typical day-to-day price swing, scaled to a yearly figure. Lower volatility = smoother ride. Income-focused ETFs typically land between 10–25 %. ✦ < 15 % low · 15–25 % moderate · > 35 % high
Max DD% (Maximum Drawdown)
The largest peak-to-trough decline in price during the period, expressed as a negative percentage. Represents the worst loss an investor who bought at the top and sold at the bottom would have suffered. ✦ Less negative is better; −10 % is better than −40 %
52w High / 52w Low
The highest and lowest unadjusted closing price over the trailing 52 weeks. Helps you gauge where today's price sits within its recent range — useful for timing buys and monitoring momentum. ✦ Price near 52w high = relative strength; near low = potential value or distress
Ann Inc (Annual Income)
Estimated annual dividend income from this holding: ($ Amount ÷ current price) × dividend per share × payments per year. Based on the most recent stored dividend data. ✦ Sum of all holdings = portfolio's estimated yearly cash flow
Mon Inc (Monthly Income)
Annual Income ÷ 12. Normalises income to a per-month figure regardless of whether the ticker pays monthly, quarterly, or weekly. Useful for budgeting cash flow. ✦ Multiply by 12 to get back to Ann Inc
Yield% (Dividend Yield)
Annual dividends per share ÷ current price × 100. A high yield can signal great income or a falling price (yield trap). Always cross-check with Ann Ret% and Max DD% to distinguish the two. ✦ > 8 % warrants extra scrutiny; check sustainability
Grade & Score
Composite score 0–100 aggregated from six metrics with these weights:
Sharpe 30 % · Sortino 20 % · Calmar 15 % · Omega 15 % · Max DD 15 % · Dn Cap 5 %.
