NAV Erosion Back-Tester

High-yield ETFs often pay large distributions while the share price slowly declines. This tool shows month-by-month how many extra shares you need to reinvest to preserve your original portfolio value — and what happens at any chosen reinvestment level (0–100%). Up to 10 years of history is supported; if the ETF doesn't go back that far, a notice will appear and results will start from the earliest available date.
Blue line = share price  ·  Green line = portfolio value  ·  Dashed gray = initial investment (break-even)

Shares Deficit = Break-Even Shares − Total Shares Held, where Break-Even Shares = Initial Investment ÷ Current Price (i.e. how many shares you'd need right now for your portfolio to equal what you originally put in). A positive (red) deficit means you're short that many shares to recover your principal at today's price — NAV erosion is winning. A negative (green) surplus means you hold more shares than break-even requires — your portfolio is already worth more than your initial investment, either from price appreciation or accumulated reinvestment.
