Risk-Adjusted Metrics

ⓘ What do these ratios mean?
Sharpe — Annualized excess return divided by total volatility (std dev). Measures return earned per unit of risk. >1.0 good, >1.5 excellent.
Sortino — Like Sharpe but only penalizes downside volatility — losses don't hurt the same as gains. >1.5 good, >2.0 excellent.
Calmar — Annualized return divided by the maximum drawdown. A Calmar of 1 means you earned back the worst drop in one year. >0.5 fair, >1.0 good.
Omega — Ratio of all gains above a threshold to all losses below it. Unlike Sharpe, uses the full return distribution, not just std dev. >1.2 fair, >1.5 good, >2.0 excellent.
Up Capture — How much of the benchmark's up-day returns this ticker captured. 100% = moves exactly with the benchmark on up days. For income ETFs 60–90% is typical and fine.
Down Capture — How much of the benchmark's down-day losses this ticker absorbed. Lower is better. <80% excellent, <90% good, >100% means it lost more than the benchmark.
